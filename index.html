Requirements:
1) I wanted a logo made from Unicode characters. This way, I could use it as a signature in the digital assets that I create and work in.
INSPIRATION: messages left on circuit boards, chinese seals2) I wanted to create the beginning of a logo system: versatile and adaptable, the start of a brand identity and visual language.
INSPIRATION: LA28, MIT Media Lab, The Branch Museum of Design, etc.3) I wanted a logo that was small, recognizable, and clean, aligning with general design guidelines.

References:
1) Royal Copenhagen's 3 blue waves, the blue and white color scheme, and their BLUELINE collection. Copenhagen has a special place in my heart.2) Fishladder: "a waterway structure on or around artificial and natural barriers (such as dams, locks and waterfalls) that provides aquatic animals (particularly fish) a 'detour' to facilitate natural movements and migrations" of species like salmon. [Wikipedia]3) Nature has always been my source of inspiration with its unlimited visual imagery and structure that informs the design of everything from the Shinkansen to Velcro.

THE LOGO:
1) 5 Unicode characters in Times New Roman font. Blends the use of a foundational system for computers to communicate with a foundational serif font for professional, legal and academic papers.2) The 1st, 4th, and 5th characters are single guillemets. Reminscient of chinese quotation marks (angle brackets).3) The 2nd character is a bullet point (U+2022). My favorite style of note-taking.4) The 3rd character is a parentheses. Used everywhere. Simple.
